Famous Temples in Bishnupur

Rasmancha

The Rasmancha is the oldest brick temple, built in 1600 by King Bir Hambir. It has an unusual elongated pyramidal tower, surrounded by hut-shaped turrets typical of Bengali roof structures of the time. It was used to display idols of Radha-Krishna during the Ras festival.

Jor Bangla Temple

The Jor Bangla Temple, also known as Keshto Ray Temple, was built by King Raghunath Singha Dev II in 1655. It features ornate terracotta carvings set off by the roof in the classic chala style of Bengal architecture.

Pancha Ratna Temple

The Pancha Ratna Temple, dedicated to Shyam Ray (Krishna), was built in 1643 by King Raghunath Singha. It stands on a low square plinth with an ambulatory pathway and consists of five towers. Madanmohan Temple

The Madanmohan Temple, built in 1694 by King Durjana Singha Deva, is dedicated to Madanmohan (Krishna). It features impressive carvings on its walls that depict scenes from the Ramayana, Mahabharata, and the Puranas.

Lalji Temple

Built in 1658 by Bir Singha II, the Lalji Temple is dedicated to Radha Krishna. It is built in the ekaranta style on a square raised plinth, featuring ornamental stucco decorations on low relief carvings.

View on Google Maps

Radhya Shyam Temple

The Radhya Shyam Temple, built in 1758 by Chaitanya Singha, is dedicated to Radha Krishna. It features a dome-shaped shikhara and stucco motifs depicting floral, geometric, and life scenes from the Puranas.

View on Google Maps

Nandalal Temple

Built in the 17th century, the Nandalal Temple is dedicated to Nandalal (Krishna). It consists of a square ground plan and a single tower resting on a curved roof, with minimal motif work on the lower part of the temple.

Kalachand Temple

Built in 1656 by King Raghunath Singha, the Kalachand Temple is constructed in the ekratna style with laterite stone.
