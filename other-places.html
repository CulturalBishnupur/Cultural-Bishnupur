Other Attractions in Bishnupur

Bishnupur Gate

The Bishnupur Gate is one of the most iconic landmarks of the city. This historical gate is a testament to the rich cultural and architectural heritage of Bishnupur.

Bishnupur Museum

The Bishnupur Museum houses a vast collection of artifacts that showcase the history, culture, and artistry of Bishnupur.

View on Google Maps

Gumghar

Gumghar is a mysterious square terracotta structure. It is known as Gumghar, perhaps the torture chamber for the criminals built during the Malla era.

View on Google Maps

Dalmadal Cannon

The Dalmadal Cannon, built under Raja Gopal Singh in 1742, was made for protection against the Maratha invasion. Built with iron, the cannon is unaffected by rain, sun, and other climatic conditions, remaining rust-free. The word 'dala' means enemy and 'mardan' means to destroy.

View on Google Maps

Lalbandh

Lalbandh is a historical lake known for its unique historical significance in the cultural heritage of Bishnupur. The lake was made for drinking water and to protect the town from enemies. Malla Raj Raghunath Singha is said to have been infatuated with a Persian dancer, Lalbai. He took her under his protection and later dug this large pond in her name, Lalbandh.

View on Google Maps

Mrinmoyee Temple

Mrinmoyee Temple, the oldest temple in Bishnupur, was established by King Jagat Malla in 997 AD. According to local history, Maa Mrinmoyee ordered the king in his dreams to build the temple. Goddess Durga is worshipped here as Maa Mrinmoyee.
